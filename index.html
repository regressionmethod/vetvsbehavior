⚡ Quick Decision Tool
🚨
EMERGENCY - GO TO VET NOW:
Difficulty breathing, seizures, unconsciousness, severe injury, suspected poisoning, bloated abdomen, or extreme lethargy.
🏥 Likely VET VISIT Needed
  • • Sudden behavior changes
  • • Physical symptoms present
  • • Age-related changes (senior dogs)
  • • Pain indicators
  • • Eating/drinking changes
🧠 Likely BEHAVIOR Issue
  • • Gradual behavior changes
  • • Triggered by environment
  • • No physical symptoms
  • • Related to schedule/routine
  • • Responds to training cues
🔍 Detailed Symptom Checker

Check all symptoms your dog is showing. We'll help you decide next steps.

🚨 High Priority - Vet Likely Needed
Sudden loss of appetite (24+ hours)
Vomiting or diarrhea (multiple episodes)
Sudden aggression or personality change
Limping or signs of pain
Excessive thirst or urination
Difficulty breathing or panting at rest
⚠️ Medium Priority - Monitor Closely
Changes in sleep patterns
Increased clinginess or withdrawal
Repetitive behaviors (spinning, tail chasing)
Decreased energy or playfulness
Senior dog (7+ years) with new behaviors
House training regression with accidents
🧠 Low Priority - Likely Behavioral
Barking at specific triggers
Jumping on people during greetings
Pulling on leash during walks
Ignoring commands they used to know
Destructive behavior when alone
Overstimulation or hyperactivity
💬 How to Talk to Your Vet
📋 What to Prepare Before the Call
• Timeline: When did behaviors start?
• Triggers: What seems to set off the behavior?
• Frequency: How often does it happen?
• Physical symptoms: Any eating, drinking, sleep changes?
• Recent changes: New food, environment, routine?
🗣️ Phone Script Example
"Hi, I'm calling about [dog's name]. They've been showing [specific behavior] for [timeframe]. I'm wondering if this could be medical or if it's behavioral. They're [age] years old and this behavior [started suddenly/gradually]. Should I schedule an exam or try behavioral approaches first?"
❓ Questions to Ask
• "Could this be pain-related?"
• "Are there medical conditions that cause this behavior?"
• "Should we do any specific tests?"
• "If it's not medical, who do you recommend for behavior help?"
